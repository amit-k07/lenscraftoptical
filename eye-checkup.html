Eye Checkup

Complete Eye Examination

At an eye checkup, you can expect a comfortable and simple process. The doctor will first ask you a few questions about your vision and overall health, such as whether you experience blurry vision, headaches, eye strain, or if you have any medical conditions like diabetes. Next, your vision will be tested using an eye chart where you’ll read letters from a distance. This helps determine how clearly you can see. The doctor may then perform a refraction test, where you look through different lenses and say which one makes your vision clearer. This helps find the correct prescription if you need glasses or contact lenses. Your eyes will also be checked for overall health. The doctor may shine a light into your eyes to examine the pupils and the front part of the eye. In some cases, eye drops are used to dilate (widen) your pupils so the back of the eye can be examined more closely. This part is painless, though your vision may stay blurry for a few hours afterward. By the end of the appointment, the doctor will explain the results and let you know if you need glasses, contact lenses, medication, or any further treatment.
